# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary: SDF hackathon jury and Stellar ecosystem developers evaluating real-world ZK use cases. Context: reviewing a submission under time pressure, judging technical credibility and product clarity.

Secondary: Startup CTOs and payroll operators considering on-chain salary infrastructure. Context: due diligence on whether private payroll on Stellar is viable without exposing individual salaries.

## Product Purpose

sobrecito is confidential payroll on Stellar/Soroban: an organization pays salaries in USDC on-chain while keeping individual amounts private, then proves with a ZK proof that the batch total is correct for the auditor. The envelope (sobre → sobrecito, "little envelope") is the metaphor: sealed for the public, openable only by the person with the right key.

Success: a visitor understands "prove without showing" in one phrase and immediately grasps why it matters. The landing is the forcing-function of clarity before circuits.

## Brand Personality

Sealed · verifiable · irreversible

Tone: high-end, premium, sober — no hype. Clarity over effect. The product is technically serious; the brand must match that register. Consistent with honest-disclosure discipline (PoC, not audited).

Emotional goals: confidence in the mechanism, not excitement about the feature. The visitor should feel they are looking at infrastructure, not marketing.

## Anti-references

- SaaS-cream: warm beige/sand backgrounds with dusty brown primary — the AI default of 2026
- Startup pitch deck aesthetic: hero metrics, gradient text, eyebrow tags on every section, "01 / 02 / 03" numbered callouts
- Hero-metric template: large stat grids, bold numbers as decoration
- Generic "dark mode" tech aesthetic: glassmorphism used decoratively, gradient-on-gradient backgrounds
- Editorial-typographic lane (2026 saturated): serif display + mono labels + ruled separators

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you prove** — the interface embodies the product's core promise: sealed by default, detail revealed only on demand. The centerpiece is not a feature description; it is a live demonstration.
2. **Weight carries hierarchy** — one typeface at extreme weight contrast (Geist 900 / 400) replaces multiple typefaces. Size separates H1 from H2; weight and color do the rest.
3. **Dark ground, luminous proof** — the tinted blue-black substrate (colored, not pure black) with a whisper of blue undertone makes the accent (electric blue) read as luminous truth. The amber accent signals exposure/danger by contrast (the one warm note against the cool field).
4. **Honest disclosure by design** — PoC status, non-audited state, and the distinction between technical guarantees and policy guarantees are part of the brand, not footnotes.
5. **Show, don't describe** — the toggle centerpiece shows the product working with fake data. The differentiation section names competitors directly. No abstract value props.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum; AAA target for body text on dark background (≥7:1 contrast for ink-on-bg). Reduced-motion alternative for all animations (instant crossfade). Touch targets minimum 44px (48px for the toggle). No color-only indicators — verified state always has both color and text/symbol.
